Design Fundamentals Year 1

UX TASK

My goal for my website was to create a page that showcased what I have learnt so far in my first year and semester at university. I have included 3 different designs for a poster focusing on contrast, alignment, repetition and proximity; the 8 systems of typography and examples of each; as well as the journey I have had so far. The target is to create the website so it can be viewed by Mary and Mic but also to show friends/family and future potential employers. It will be a scrolling website with all the information on the one page, it will have multiple images to show the different things I have learned as well as being examples. I want to make this project successful by having everything in it that is required, making it visually pleasing, and work smoothly.

I am hopefully going to make this a portfolio piece and continue adding to the website after it has been submitted to show off my work, as well as gradually improving the website to include new techniques. I will hopefully then be able to use this as a portfolio piece for future employers to view to see my work.

Moodboard

I have selected a few images to get inspiration from when designing my website, I am wanting to have a simplistic look so I was looking at different websites which reflected this and used this to try and come up with my own design for the website. I used Adobe Colour to help make a colour scheme up, I have got other pallettes in the moodboard that I had not proceeded with but feel it is useful to show the thought process that went through the deisgn process. I feel that going with black and white tones with a singular other colour helps keep the website simplistic yet professional.
Although optional, I feel a moodboard can be very helpful before designing the page as it helps me get a clear idea of the direction I want to go in. It is a good thing to do as it gets you in the flow of who and what you are designing for. It is helpful for when you are desigining for a specific target market which is not usually your own style it can help you keep on track for what is required.

Flowchart/Wireframe

Originally I had wanted to do a scrolling page with everything on the front page, however after completing all the worksheets that were set out I found that making multiple pages was just as easy (Infact maybe a bit easier). The images on the left show my old and new flowchart. My original idea is on the left and my original idea on how I wanted to lay the page out, and the one on the right is closer to the final project. I am much happier with the design now as I feel it is more professional and sleek looking. I wanted to include buttons on each page so the user can flick between the different pages with ease. I wanted a simplistic look and didnt want to overcomplicate the project by including a vast array of colours and designs. I feel that by keeping the same format for each page makes it easier to navigate for the users, as I would like my family to look at the project once I have submitted it. On the right is a wireframe of the final website, It doesnt include all of the pages as I have added more in since creating the wireframe. However it shows the layout of each page and the structure of how it is going to look.
