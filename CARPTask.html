C.A.R.P. TASK

C.A.R.P. stands for Contrast, Alignment, Repetition and Proximity. The following posters have been included in the design of these posters. I have designed 3 different posters for the True/False Festival in Misouri. I have used Adobe Indesign to create these 3 advertisement pieces, although tricky at first I now have a basic understanding of the software.

Contrast means that there are 2 elements that are vastly different to make them each stand out in their own way. This can be with shapes, colours, size or textures. Alignment is when items on the page are organised in a way where everything conects to each other. It can be aligned along different shapes or with each other. Repetition is exactly how It sounds it can be a repeat of a certain aspect in the design, this can be anything on the design as long as it is repeated. Proximity is to do with how close elements are in a piece also the white space that is created on the page. This helps with the flow of the design and show what is more important.

I have created a more simplistic look with this poster to try and show off the images a bit more. I have used a colour for the background that compliments both the images. I have centred everything down the middle except the logo and website in the bottom right. This keeps symmetry across the page and everything looking tidy. I have repeated the same font throughout the page and kept with the white text, I have created contract by using the large text next to the small text showing hierarchy. There is some space on the outside of the poster to highlight the important information in the middle as well as making the poster simplistic.
This poster has got a lot of things crammed into the page; it has got 5 images down the right-hand side showing the different things with the festival. I have kept a similar colour scheme on the left-hand side and tried to use a colour that can be reflected in the images on the right. I decided to use a gradient as I could not find an exact colour that would work. I have used contrast with the dark background and white text and have centre aligned the text with each other to create a uniformed look. I like this poster and it is the one I have spent more time on. I have kept the colours from the logo and designed the poster around this. The red in the image represents both an T and an F to symbolise this from the festival. I have aligned the text along the edges of the letter so that it all flows in a geometrical pattern. I have used white space at the bottom of the page so that It gives emphasis on the information in the centre of the poster.
